Physarum polycephalum is a slime mold with no neurons that solves network optimization problems. Each agent senses the chemoattractant trail ahead-left and ahead-right, rotates toward the stronger signal, moves forward, and deposits more trail. Repeated 120,000 times per frame.

The emergent networks approximate Steiner minimum trees — connecting all food nodes with near-minimal total path length. This is NP-hard to solve exactly. The organism finds near-optimal solutions in minutes through pure chemistry.
