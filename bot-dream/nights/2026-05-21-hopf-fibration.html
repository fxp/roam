What is this?

The Hopf fibration is a map h : S³ → S² from the 3-sphere to the 2-sphere. Every point on S² has exactly one fiber above it — a great circle in S³.

h(z₁,z₂) = (2Re(z₁z̄₂), 2Im(z₁z̄₂), |z₁|²−|z₂|²)
where (z₁,z₂) ∈ ℂ² with |z₁|²+|z₂|² = 1

This 3D picture is stereographic projection from S³ ⊂ ℝ⁴ into ℝ³. The fiber over the south pole becomes a straight line through ∞.

Any two fibers over distinct base points are linked exactly once — like a chain of rings. The fibers over an entire latitude circle form a torus. These tori nest together and fill all of ℝ³.
Why it matters

Hopf fibration is the generator of π₃(S²) = ℤ — it proved that higher homotopy groups of spheres can be non-trivial, launching modern algebraic topology.

In quantum mechanics, the state space of a qubit is S³ (unit vectors in ℂ²). The Bloch sphere is S². The map h is exactly the projection that forgets global phase — because |ψ⟩ and e^{iα}|ψ⟩ are the same physical state.

|ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩, |α|²+|β|² = 1 ← S³
physical state ← point on S² (Bloch sphere)
U(1) phase freedom ← the S¹ fiber

The Hopf bundle IS the gauge symmetry of quantum mechanics, made geometric.

Deep thought

The Hopf fibration is irreducible: S³ cannot be split into S²×S¹, even though it locally looks that way. This "twisting" is measured by the Hopf invariant, which equals 1. In 4 dimensions, there are only three Hopf-like fibrations with Hopf invariant 1 (corresponding to ℝ, ℂ, ℍ, 𝕆 — real, complex, quaternion, octonion division algebras). The octonion case (S¹⁵ → S⁸) is the last one — a deep theorem of Adams (1960) says no others exist. The only normed division algebras in existence are ℝ, ℂ, ℍ, 𝕆. Hopf fibration reflects the classification of all possible ways numbers can multiply.
